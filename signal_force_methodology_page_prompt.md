# 5. The Seven Step Intelligence Cycle

Keep the current intelligence cycle but make it more productized and visually tied to the AI workflow.

Title:
From raw federal signal to prioritized BD action.

Intro:
Signal Force adapts intelligence cycle discipline to federal growth. Each cycle starts with a decision requirement and ends with a usable action plan.

Use a horizontal timeline, radial loop, or interactive stepper.

Steps:

01 / Requirements
Define the decision. What does the team need to know? What agency, market, opportunity, prime, technology, or relationship question is driving the work?

02 / Collection
Gather the signal. Pull from federal data, opportunity sources, agency material, budget movement, contract history, news, company context, people data, and relationship inputs.

03 / Processing
Normalize and structure the data. Clean the language, classify the signals, group the entities, resolve companies and people, and prepare the information for analysis.

04 / Correlation
Connect the layers. Tie money to mission, mission to agency demand, demand to opportunity movement, opportunity to incumbents, incumbents to teaming paths, and teaming paths to people.

05 / Analysis
Use AI and operator logic to identify patterns, gaps, leverage, timing, risk, and likely next moves. Separate signal from noise.

06 / Production
Generate the read, brief, map, matrix, or action plan. Keep it sourced, structured, calibrated, and decision ready.

07 / Feedback
Refine the model based on what the user does, what the market does next, and which assumptions prove right or wrong.

Add a small callout:
The cycle is not a content workflow. It is a decision workflow.
