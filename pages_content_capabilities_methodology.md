## The Reads

**Code:** R / READS
**Eyebrow:** The Outputs
**Title:** Same engine. Different reads. *All decision-ready.*

**Intro:**
Every read is a workflow the engine runs, not a separate product. Each one is built around a real BD decision — what to chase, who to talk to, when to move, when to wait.

### R-01 — Agency Fit Read
- **Decision:** Should we target this agency?
- **Signal Force analyzes:** Agency mission, budget priorities, active programs, modernization themes, recent awards, incumbent landscape, technology demand, and company fit.
- **You walk away with:** A focused agency fit brief showing where the company has a credible angle, where it does not, and what to do next.

### R-02 — Opportunity Qualification Read
- **Decision:** Is this opportunity worth pursuing?
- **Signal Force analyzes:** Customer profile, incumbent standing, recompete window, vehicle and set-aside fit, likely competitor team, and buying-language pattern match.
- **You walk away with:** A pursue / no-pursue brief with sourced evidence, calibrated confidence, and a clear recommendation.

### R-03 — Teaming Strategy Read
- **Decision:** Should we team, prime, or pass?
- **Signal Force analyzes:** Prime teaming behavior, recent sub selections, post-award teaming paths, capability overlap, and Shipley-aligned teaming logic.
- **You walk away with:** A teaming brief with named partner targets, the fastest path to revenue, and the outreach approach for each.

### R-04 — Market Signal Read
- **Decision:** Where is the market moving — and where can we lead?
- **Signal Force analyzes:** Technology demand signals across agencies, budget priority alignment, modernization theme uptake, and procurement-pattern recognition.
- **You walk away with:** A forward-looking market signal brief showing where demand is forming and where the company has a credible position to lead.

### R-05 — BD Action Plan
- **Decision:** What do we do next week — and next month?
- **Signal Force analyzes:** Everything above plus the relationship layer, capture-cycle alignment, and operator priorities.
- **You walk away with:** A 7-day and 30-day named-action plan: who calls whom, which doors to push, which pursuits to advance, which to drop.

## Four-Layer Model

**Code:** 02 / FOUR-LAYER MODEL
**Eyebrow:** The Analytical Model
**Title:** Any single layer is commodity. *The cross-correlation is the IP.*

**Intro:**
Signal Force synthesizes across four layers. A single award notice becomes a signal about customer intent, the winner's positioning, teaming vulnerabilities, talent flow, and the next award in the same domain.

### L-01 — Money
**Blurb:** Contract value, vehicle, period of performance, agency, NAICS, set-aside, obligations, modifications.

**Detail:** Tracks the full money flow — budget formulation, forecasts, RFIs, solicitation, award, modifications, performance, and recompete windows. Most platforms see the middle; Signal Force sees the leading and trailing indicators.

**Signal sources:** Award notices · Mods + option exercises · Recompete windows · Budget POMs · CR exposure

### L-02 — Capability
**Blurb:** Which firms can actually do this work — past performance, cleared bench, vehicles, geography, technology.

**Detail:** Maps the supply side of every market: who has the past performance, who holds the right vehicles, who has the cleared bench, who has the geographic footprint, and who has been quietly acquiring capability.

**Signal sources:** Past performance · Vehicle holdings · Cleared bench · M&A activity · Tech capability shifts

### L-03 — People
**Blurb:** BD, capture, and program leaders — where they have been, where they are going, and who they trust.

**Detail:** The relationship layer. Tracks BD and capture leadership at primes and subs, recent moves, capture leads behind specific wins, hiring patterns, and warm-intro paths into target customers and partners.

**Signal sources:** BD moves · Capture leads · Hiring surges · Network paths · Trade-press announcements

### L-04 — Mission
**Blurb:** What the customer is actually trying to accomplish — pain points, technology shifts, political pressure.

**Detail:** The customer-truth layer. Decodes mission language from industry days, RFIs, strategic plans, and policy shifts. Translates buying language patterns into the real procurement intent behind the dollars.

**Signal sources:** Industry day themes · RFI language drift · Modernization mandates · Policy shocks · Program office moves

**Synthesis diagram:** Money / Capability / People / Mission → SYNTHESIS. The four layer nodes converge into a single synthesis node; the active layer glows and traces a line into synthesis.

## Question Engine

**Code:** QE / QUESTION ENGINE
**Eyebrow:** Standing requirements + ad-hoc requests
**Title:** Customers don't passively receive intelligence. *They task it.*

**Intro:**
Two channels feed every customer's intelligence base — Standing Priority Intelligence Requirements set during onboarding, and ad-hoc Requests for Intelligence answered on demand. Together they form the Question Engine.

### Standing PIRs — Continuous collection against named questions
Set during onboarding, reviewed quarterly. Each PIR drives a collection plan, a scheduled analytical review, and inclusion in routine intelligence products.

- » Track NGA cyber recompetes 12-24 months out.
- » Monitor SAIC and Leidos BD leadership for moves.
- » Surface AI / ML procurement signals across DoD.
- » Watch sources sought in our target NAICS codes.

### Ad-hoc RFIs — Specific questions answered on demand
Combination of automated analysis against Signal Force's intelligence base and operator-led research. Premium tiers get 48-72 hour turnaround on substantive RFIs.

- » Who is likely teaming on the next SEWP recompete?
- » What is the capture posture on USDA modernization?
- » Right capture lead to target at SAIC for IC modernization?
- » Our exposure if the CR continues through Q3?
